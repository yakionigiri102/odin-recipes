Japanese Curry Recipe

Description

Japanese curry rice is very easy to make and a very popular dish in Japan. Unlike other curries, Japanese people don’t mix up all sorts of different spices, but use store bought curry roux. Because they are so popular, there are many different varieties of curry roux available in Japan.

Ingredients

  • House Vermont Curry (medium hot)
  • Cooked rice
  • Potato – cut to 1.5cm / ⅝” cubes
  • Carrot – sliced to 7mm / ¼” thick pieces
  • Chicken Cutlet (Japanese Chicken Schnitzel) – cut into 2.5cm / 1″ wide strips
  • Fukujinzuke as a condiment (if you have it)

Steps

  1. Put oil into a pot and sauté onion pieces until the edges start browning
  2. Add Potato and Carrot pieces to the pot and stir.
  3. Add water and bring it to a boil. Remove scum as it rises.
  4. Add curry roux and cook.
  5. Put Cooked rice on one side of a serving plate, place cutlet pieces next to the rice.
  6. Pour curry on the side next to the cutlet away from the rice.
  7. Place a small amount of Fukujinzuke on the plate.
